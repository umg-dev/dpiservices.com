Captive Insurance is not a Tax Shelter.

Its main objective is to manage risk and change an expense into a profit center.

  • What is a captive?

    What is a captive?

    It is a property casualty insurance company and it is formed to cover only the risks of the parent company. The Oxford Captive Structure only covers enterprise risks that are low frequency but high severity. Examples could be: product contamination, business interruption, product recall, mechanical breakdown, transit risks, crop insurance, etc.

    How does a Captive work?

  • What are the primary benefits of a Captive?

    What are the primary benefits of a Captive?

    The objective is to manage risks and change an expense into a profit center by combining a captive with your commercial insurance.

    Premiums paid to the captives are tax deductible, if reasonable, by the parent company. Premiums received by the captive are not taxable as well as underwriting profits until distributed. A captive must invest like an insurance company.

    Ancillary Benefits:
    1. Captive could be a subsidiary of the primary business.
    2. Builds up the balance sheet.
    3. Ownership of the captive off balance sheet / works with an asset protection structure.
    4. Ownership could provide succession planning strategies for the next generation, or provide benefits to key employees.
